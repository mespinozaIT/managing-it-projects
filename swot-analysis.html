SWOT Analysis

Strategic Self-Assessment for Project Success

üéØ What is SWOT Analysis?

SWOT Analysis is one of the most powerful strategic planning tools in project management. Originally developed for business strategy, it has become essential for understanding your current state before planning your future state.

SWOT = Strengths ‚Ä¢ Weaknesses ‚Ä¢ Opportunities ‚Ä¢ Threats

A structured framework for evaluating internal capabilities and external factors that could impact your project's success.

üí° Key Purpose: SWOT helps you make strategic decisions by providing a realistic, honest view of your current situation. It's about authentic self-assessment, not just listing what sounds impressive.

SWOT Analysis Matrix
STRENGTHS
Internal factors that give you an advantage
Ask yourself:
  • What do I do well?
  • What unique resources do I have?
  • What do others see as my strengths?
  • What achievements am I proud of?
WEAKNESSES
Internal factors that could hold you back
Ask yourself:
  • What could I improve?
  • Where do I lack resources?
  • What do others likely see as weaknesses?
  • What habits limit my success?
OPPORTUNITIES
External factors you can leverage
Ask yourself:
  • What trends could benefit me?
  • What new tools/technologies exist?
  • What connections could help?
  • What gaps can I fill?
THREATS
External factors that could cause problems
Ask yourself:
  • What obstacles do I face?
  • What is my competition doing?
  • What trends could hurt me?
  • What external factors worry me?

üéØ Objective

Complete a personal or professional SWOT analysis to build self-awareness and make more informed, strategic decisions. This sets the stage for defining clear, realistic strategies and compelling project objectives in future assignments.

üìä STRENGTHS
What are you good at? What advantages do you have? Think skills, knowledge, resources, relationships.
üîç WEAKNESSES
What skills, habits, or gaps could hold you back? Be honest about areas for improvement.
üöÄ OPPORTUNITIES

üìã Expectations for Full Credit

  • Complete all four quadrants (S, W, O, T) with thoughtful analysis
  • Include at least 3 bullet points per section with specific examples
  • Write in your own words - be honest and specific, not generic
  • Show self-reflection that demonstrates genuine introspection
  • Connect to your goals - relate to your mission and vision from previous activities
  • Distinguish internal vs. external factors clearly (S&W are internal, O&T are external)

Scoring: Each section (S/W/O/T) is worth 5 points. Quality matters more than quantity.
