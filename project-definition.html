Project Definition

What Makes Something a Project?

Four Key Characteristics of Projects

A temporary endeavor
Projects are not ongoing operations—they aim to deliver value or solve a problem.
Goal-driven and outcome-focused
PM ensures teams don't just stay busy—they stay aligned with business value.
A collaborative effort
PMs lead diverse teams, navigate personalities, and ensure communication.
Unique
Aims to produce a unique outcome, which can be a product, service, or result.

Professional IT Project Examples

Launching a company website redesign
PM Concepts Illustrated:
Involves scope planning, stakeholder input, timelines, budgets, and managing developers/designers.
Rolling out a new HR software system
PM Concepts Illustrated:
Cross-functional coordination between IT, HR, and vendors; data migration; training and change management.
Organizing a student research conference
PM Concepts Illustrated:
Scheduling, venue booking, marketing, volunteer coordination, managing deliverables on a deadline.
Upgrading classroom tech at a university
PM Concepts Illustrated:
Requires vendor management, budgeting, testing equipment, and coordinating installs during breaks.

Personal Project Examples

Planning a graduation trip
Temporary, goal-driven, has a budget and timeline
PM Concepts Involved:
Scope (where to go), Budget, Timeline, Risk (flight delays), Stakeholders (friends/family)
Applying to grad school
Multi-step, deadline-based, outcome-focused
PM Concepts Involved:
Milestones (test prep, applications), Time management, Document control, Task tracking
Launching a personal website or portfolio
Requires planning, content creation, and tools
PM Concepts Involved:
Scope, Requirements, Scheduling, Tool selection, Iteration
Organizing a family reunion
Coordinating people, places, and preferences
PM Concepts Involved:
Communication plan, Budget, Risk (weather), Resource management
Moving into a new apartment
One-time, logistical, deadline-driven
PM Concepts Involved:
Checklist, Budget, Dependencies (utilities, movers), Procurement
Preparing for a professional certification (e.g., PMP, AWS)
Time-boxed with a clear outcome
PM Concepts Involved:
Timeline, Study plan (Work Breakdown Structure), Self-monitoring (status reports), Risk (burnout)

Remember

Projects are everywhere! Understanding these characteristics helps you recognize when project management principles can make your work—both professional and personal—more successful.
