Course Description

University Catalog Description: Effective behavioral and technical methods used in managing information systems development over the project life cycle.

🎯 Course Structure Overview

This course utilizes the Project Management Institute (PMI) Project Management Principles and Knowledge Areas as the foundation, supplemented with real-world industry experience. While the outlined schedule provides structure and learning objectives, the course will follow an agile methodology approach, allowing for iterative adjustments based on student needs, learning pace, and class dynamics.

Learning Outcomes

Upon successful completion of this course, students will be able to:

1
Apply project management principles in context by adapting PMI-aligned frameworks to address real-world organizational challenges and dynamic project environments.
2
Design and deliver a project that reflects individual goals and professional relevance, incorporating strategic planning, collaboration, and critical reflection.
3
Develop core project management artifacts—including a project charter, work breakdown structure (WBS), communication plan, and risk register—aligned with organizational objectives and stakeholder expectations.
4
Demonstrate leadership and collaboration skills through active participation in team-based work, utilizing emotional intelligence, effective communication, and shared accountability.
5
Communicate effectively with diverse stakeholders, including technical and non-technical audiences, through clear written documentation, structured presentations, and purposeful dialogue.
6
Analyze key domains of Information Technology—such as infrastructure, software development, data, and security—to support informed decision-making and cross-functional coordination.
7
Utilize AI tools and digital platforms in a responsible and reflective manner, enhancing creativity, organization, and learning throughout the project lifecycle.
