Definition Box

Project Management is the application of knowledge, skills, tools, and techniques to project activities to meet project requirements.

Characteristics Grid

A temporary endeavor
Projects are not ongoing operations—they aim to deliver value or solve a problem.
Goal-driven and outcome-focused
PM ensures teams don't just stay busy—they stay aligned with business value.

Musts Grid (PM Definition Page)

Must Have Clear Goals

Every project must have defined objectives and success criteria.

Must Have Timeline

Projects require specific start and end dates with milestones.

Must Have Resources

Adequate budget, people, and tools are essential for success.

Element Cards

👥 People

Project teams, stakeholders, and sponsors who drive project success through collaboration and expertise.

NOT Section

What Project Management is NOT

Not Just Task Management

PM is more than creating to-do lists and checking off completed items.

Not Micromanagement

Effective PMs empower teams rather than control every detail.

Skills Grid (Project Manager Role Page)

Technical Skills
Understanding of technical concepts, tools, and methodologies relevant to the project domain.
Leadership Skills
Ability to guide and motivate team members toward project objectives.
Communication Skills
Clear, effective communication with stakeholders at all levels of the organization.

Manager Cards (PM vs Functional)

👨‍💼 Functional Manager
Responsible for ongoing operations, managing permanent staff, and maintaining business-as-usual activities within a specific functional area.
🎯 Project Manager
Leads temporary teams to deliver specific project outcomes within defined time, cost, and quality constraints.

PMO Types Grid

🏢 Directive PMO

High control level. PMO manages projects directly and project managers report directly to the PMO.

📊 Controlling PMO

Moderate control level. PMO provides support and requires compliance with project management standards.

📚 Supportive PMO

Low control level. PMO provides consultative role, templates, best practices, and training.

Common Misconceptions

❌ "PM is just task management"
Project management involves strategic thinking, stakeholder management, and value delivery - not just tracking tasks.
❌ "Anyone can be a PM"
Effective project management requires specific skills, knowledge, and experience to navigate complex challenges.
❌ "PM is about micromanaging"
Good PMs empower teams and focus on removing obstacles rather than controlling every detail.
