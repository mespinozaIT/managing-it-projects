Lessons Learned (and What I'm Doing Differently)

I believe in continuous improvement. Here's what I've learned from previous semesters and how I'm making this course even better.

üìÑ
More Structured Artifacts (With Flexibility)
In past semesters, asking students to "go find" templates (like status reports) led to unnecessary confusion.
This time: I'll provide more standardized core documents ‚Äî including what they should achieve and key components ‚Äî while still challenging you to build and adapt your own when appropriate.
üë•
Better Balance Between Group and Individual Work
Previously, starting group work too early ‚Äî and running it too long ‚Äî sometimes meant individual voices got lost.
This time, you'll have:
  • Opportunities to contribute independently early on
  • A better balance between solo and group tasks
  • Clear checkpoints for individual accountability
üîÑ
More Dynamic Collaboration Opportunities
When students only worked within one group all semester, deep relationships formed ‚Äî but cross-group interaction suffered.
This time: While you'll still have a main project team, I'll also create chances for temporary groups and mixed-team discussions, encouraging cross-team learning throughout the semester.
üó£
Improved Feedback Loops
Class-wide feedback can feel vague or scattered.
So now I'll:
  • Use breakouts for small-group reflection
  • Ask each group to select a representative to share their insights
  • Validate and synthesize those ideas together as a class
"This course is a shared journey, and you'll have a voice in how we shape it."
