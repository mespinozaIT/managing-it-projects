A Personal Example: My Professional SWOT

To show you how this works authentically, let me share my honest assessment combining my CliftonStrengths results with my IT capabilities. This demonstrates how you can use multiple data sources to create a comprehensive SWOT analysis.

Strategic Insights from My Combined SWOT:

Strengths: My Significance + Focus strengths align perfectly with my high PM and development lifecycle skills. I naturally want to do important work and can execute with discipline.

Weaknesses: Low infrastructure and cybersecurity skills, plus I can be rigid when plans change (low adaptability from my bottom strengths).

Opportunities: My Futuristic strength helps me stay ahead of IT trends, while my PM expertise lets me teach others effectively.

Threats: Fast-changing security landscape requires partnerships, and my focus on significance might make me impatient with "routine" tasks.

This combined assessment shows how personality strengths align with technical skills - and where they don't! My natural desire for significant work (Significance) matches my strongest technical areas (PM, development lifecycle), but I need to partner with others for infrastructure and security expertise.

1

3

4

5
Strategic Focus
This analysis directly informs career decisions, project choices, and professional development priorities.

üéØ Practical Application

This SWOT analysis directly influenced my career choices: I focus on PM and development lifecycle roles where my strengths shine, while partnering with infrastructure and security experts for areas where I'm weak. This is exactly the kind of strategic insight your SWOT analysis should provide - actionable direction for your career and project choices.
